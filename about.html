About CNAB

A joint centre advancing research at the intersection of nutrition, biochemistry, and the biosciences.

Our story

History & rationale

The Centre for Nutraceutical and Applied Biosciences (CNAB) was established as a joint academic initiative within the Department of Biochemistry and Molecular Biology at the University of Chittagong. It brings together two long-standing research programmes — one focused on food science and nutraceuticals, the other on applied biosciences — under a shared administrative and intellectual umbrella.

The centre was created to strengthen cross-laboratory collaboration, provide a unified identity for graduate training, and offer a single point of contact for external partners interested in working with the department's experimental and computational bioscience groups.

Vision

Our vision

To be a recognised centre of excellence in nutraceutical and applied bioscience research in Bangladesh, producing rigorous, reproducible science that contributes to public health, food security, and the training of the next generation of bioscientists.

Mission

Our mission

To conduct high-quality, interdisciplinary research on food-derived bioactives and applied biological systems; to train graduate students in modern experimental and computational methods; and to build sustainable collaborations with national and international partners.

Why a joint centre

Two laboratories, one shared ecosystem

Nutrition science and applied bioscience are deeply interconnected: the bioactivity of dietary compounds can only be understood through molecular and computational tools, while applied biological questions often find their most meaningful context in human nutrition and food systems. CNAB exists to make those connections explicit.

By operating as a joint centre, the two laboratories share core instrumentation, graduate seminars, journal clubs, and administrative support — while retaining independent research identities, separate principal investigators, and distinct scientific programmes.

Structure

Organisational structure

CNAB operates under the Department of Biochemistry and Molecular Biology, with two independent laboratories reporting through their respective principal investigators.
