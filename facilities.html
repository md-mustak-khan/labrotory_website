Facilities & Equipment

Core instruments and shared research resources available to CNAB researchers.

Core instruments

Major equipment

HPLC system photo placeholder

High-Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC)

Used for separation, identification, and quantification of bioactive compounds in food and botanical extracts.

UV-Vis spectrophotometer photo placeholder

UV-Vis Spectrophotometer

Routine quantitative analysis of proteins, nucleic acids, and colourimetric assays across both laboratories.

Real-time PCR instrument photo placeholder

Real-Time PCR (qPCR) System

Gene expression analysis, pathogen detection, and validation of sequencing results.

Gel documentation system photo placeholder

Gel Documentation System

Imaging and analysis of agarose and polyacrylamide gels for molecular biology workflows.

Rotary evaporator photo placeholder

Rotary Evaporator

Concentration of extracts and solvents under reduced pressure for phytochemistry work.

Microplate reader photo placeholder

Microplate Reader

Absorbance- and fluorescence-based assays for antioxidant capacity, enzyme activity, and cell viability.

Shared resources

Shared resources

Computing cluster

Shared high-performance computing resources for bioinformatics, sequence analysis, and reproducible research workflows.

Cell culture facility

Dedicated tissue-culture space with biosafety cabinets, CO₂ incubators, and inverted microscopy for in-vitro studies.

Sample preparation lab

Shared space for extraction, fractionation, and sample preparation for both food chemistry and molecular biology workflows.

Cold storage & biobank

Ultra-low temperature freezers and organised sample storage for long-term preservation of biological and extract samples.

CNAB

The Centre for Nutraceutical and Applied Biosciences (CNAB) is a joint academic research centre within the Department of Biochemistry and Molecular Biology, University of Chittagong, Bangladesh.
