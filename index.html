Voices of the two laboratories

Short reflections from the principal investigators guiding CNAB's research culture, training, and scientific ambition.

Food Science & Nutraceutical

Prof. Dr. Dwaipayan Sikdar

Our laboratory is committed to studying how food-derived compounds interact with biological systems in ways that are both scientifically rigorous and meaningful for human health. We focus on understanding bioactive molecules, their mechanisms, and their potential contributions to nutrition and therapeutics. Every project in our group is designed not only to generate data, but also to create knowledge that can inform better food choices, public health strategies, and future interventions. We believe that translational nutraceutical science requires patience, precision, and a deep respect for the complexity of natural systems. Through collaborative work and careful experimentation, we hope to build a stronger bridge between fundamental discovery and practical benefit.

Applied Biosciences

Assoc. Prof. Dr. Suman Mojumder

Our laboratory works at the intersection of molecular biology, computation, and applied biotechnology, where questions are often complex and solutions demand both creativity and discipline. We are interested in uncovering how biological systems behave at the molecular level and how that understanding can support innovation in health, biotechnology, and environmental research. By combining experimental approaches with data-driven analysis, we aim to generate insights that are both robust and useful. We also place strong value on training young researchers to think critically, work reproducibly, and communicate science clearly. In this way, our laboratory seeks to contribute not only to science itself, but also to the next generation of bioscientists.
