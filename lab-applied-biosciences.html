Laboratory of Applied Biosciences

Applying molecular biology, bioinformatics, and computational approaches to questions in gene function and biotechnology.

Principal Investigator

Dr. Suman Mojumder

Associate Professor, Department of Biochemistry and Molecular Biology, University of Chittagong

At the Laboratory of Applied Biosciences, our work focuses on using Caenorhabditis elegans as a model organism to evaluate the biological effects of plant-derived extracts and synthetic compounds. The simplicity and well-characterized biology of C. elegans make it an exceptionally valuable tool for screening therapeutic potential and understanding mechanisms that may relate to health and disease. Through this system, we aim to identify bioactive compounds that could contribute to future pharmacological discovery and translational research.

Our laboratory integrates experimental biology with modern analytical and computational approaches to strengthen model organism research in Bangladesh. By linking fundamental biological insight with applied discovery, we seek to support the development of new knowledge in drug discovery, toxicology, and human health. This work also helps build a stronger research culture in the region by encouraging rigorous, reproducible, and interdisciplinary scientific training.

Core research themes

  • Model Organism StudiesUse of C. elegans to evaluate biological effects of plant-derived extracts and synthetic compounds.
  • Bioactivity ScreeningIdentification of compounds with therapeutic relevance through robust biological assay systems.
  • Mechanistic BiologyInvestigation of pathways and responses linked to health, stress, and disease processes.
  • Applied BiotechnologyTranslation of molecular and cellular findings into broader scientific and biomedical applications.
  • Computational BiologyIntegration of data analysis and bioinformatics into experimental biological workflows.
  • Capacity BuildingTraining young researchers in reproducible, modern, and interdisciplinary bioscience methods.
