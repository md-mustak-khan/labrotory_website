Laboratory of Food Science and Nutraceutical

Investigating the biochemical basis of food-derived bioactives and evidence-based nutraceutical interventions.

Principal Investigator

Dr. Dwaipayan Sikdar

Professor, Department of Biochemistry and Molecular Biology, University of Chittagong

Dr. Sikdar leads the Laboratory of Food Science and Nutraceutical within CNAB. His research focuses on the isolation, characterisation, and biological evaluation of bioactive compounds from traditional and under-studied food sources, with an emphasis on antioxidant mechanisms, phytochemistry, and translational nutraceutical science.

He supervises graduate students at the MS and PhD levels and collaborates with partners across Bangladesh and internationally on projects linking food chemistry to human health outcomes.

Research

Research focus areas

  • Phytochemistry of traditional foodsIsolation and structural characterisation of secondary metabolites from indigenous fruits, vegetables, and spices of Bangladesh.
  • Antioxidant mechanismsCellular and biochemical studies on the radical-scavenging and cytoprotective properties of dietary polyphenols.
  • Nutraceutical developmentTranslational work on standardised botanical extracts with potential applications in metabolic and age-related conditions.
  • Food bioactivity & stabilityEffects of processing, storage, and formulation on the retention of bioactive compounds in food matrices.
  • Dietary fibre & gut healthCharacterisation of fibre fractions from local cereals and legumes and their prebiotic potential.
  • Safety & qualityEvaluation of heavy metals, pesticide residues, and adulteration in commercially available nutraceutical products.

Current projects

Bioactive profile of selected indigenous fruits

Systematic profiling of polyphenols, carotenoids, and vitamin content in under-studied native fruits, with assessment of antioxidant capacity across ripening stages.

Standardisation of a botanical extract

Development of a reproducible extraction and quality-control protocol for a traditionally used botanical, including marker compound quantification.

Food Science & Nutraceutical

Cellular cytoprotection by dietary flavonoids

In-vitro investigation of the protective effects of selected flavonoids against oxidative stress in cultured cell lines.
