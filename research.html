Research

Combined research themes, active projects, and funded grants across CNAB's two laboratories.

Overview

A shared research agenda

CNAB's research spans the full spectrum from field and bench work in food chemistry and phytochemistry to computational analyses of genomes and microbial systems. What unites our work is a commitment to rigorous methodology, open science, and research questions with tangible relevance to human health and the environment in Bangladesh and the wider region.

Research themes

Core research areas

Our research is organized around six interconnected themes that bridge phytochemistry, nutrition, molecular biology, bioinformatics, microbiology, and in vivo functional studies.

1. Phytochemistry and Nutraceutical Research

We focus on the isolation, characterization, and quantification of phytochemicals from natural sources, with an emphasis on identifying bioactive compounds with nutraceutical potential and developing functional foods and plant-derived health-promoting products.

2. Antioxidant and Phytochemical Analysis

Our work includes both qualitative and quantitative profiling of phytochemicals, along with evaluation of antioxidant capacity using biochemical and chemical assays to understand the relationship between phytochemical composition and biological activity.

3. Bioinformatics and Computational Drug Discovery

We apply computer-aided drug design, network pharmacology, molecular docking, virtual screening, and in silico pharmacokinetic analyses to identify promising bioactive compounds and elucidate their molecular targets and mechanisms of action.

4. Molecular Biology and Functional Genomics

Our laboratory investigates gene expression and molecular mechanisms underlying responses to natural products, with particular interest in stress response, metabolism, and disease-related pathways.

5. Insect Gut Microbiology and Enzymology

We characterize microbial communities associated with insect gut ecosystems and study digestive and metabolic enzyme activities through microbiological and molecular approaches.

6. In Vivo Functional Studies

Using Caenorhabditis elegans as a model organism, we evaluate the biological activity of antioxidant and bioactive compounds by examining oxidative stress responses, lifespan, and stress-resistance mechanisms.
